Lignin Nanoparticles as Pickering Stabilizers: Emulsion Engineering through Physicochemical Design

Ronald Marquez1, Roberto J. Aguado1, Quim Tarrés1, Laura Tolosa2, Blaise L. Tardy3,*, Orlando J. Rojas4,5,6,*, Martin A. Hubbe7,*, Marc Delgado Aguilar1,* (Submitted to Advances in Colloid and Interface Science)

Abstract

This critical review systematically examines the key physicochemical parameters influencing the formation, stability, and properties of Pickering emulsions stabilized by lignin nanoparticles (LNPs), considering oil fraction, nanoparticle size, concentration, surface charge, and emulsification variables (pH and shearing method). We demonstrate the broad applicability of fundamental physical chemistry principles in explaining the long-term stability of LNP-stabilized Pickering emulsions. Several important correlations were identified: (1) smaller LNPs or higher LNP concentrations led to smaller emulsion droplets and improved stability; (2) with few exceptions, emulsions stabilized by LNPs ranging from 25–50 nm remained stable for over six months at concentrations between 0.2 and 2 wt.%; (3) LNPs larger than 1 µm produced droplets exceeding 25 µm, accompanied by reduced stability; (4) emulsions were more stable at acidic to neutral pH values (pH 3–7), with some formulations at pH 6 remaining stable for over three months with minimal sedimentation, whereas complete phase separation was sometimes observed at pH 3; (5) greater negative zeta potentials (up to −64 mV) correlated with enhanced colloidal stability due to the electrostatic repulsion between particles; (6) chemical modifications such as acetylation and polymer grafting significantly enhanced emulsion stability by balancing LNP surface hydrophilicity and interfacial activity; and (7) high-energy emulsification methods, including ultrasonication and high-shear mixing, produced smaller droplets and more stable emulsions. Based on these insights, we propose practical heuristics to tailor LNP-stabilized Pickering emulsions for emerging applications requiring enhanced stability and functionality, including agriculture and crop protection, food additives, nutraceuticals, stimuli-responsive formulations, energy systems, and advanced packaging coatings.
